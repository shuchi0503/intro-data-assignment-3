Decline in Christmas Tree Harvest Across the U.S., USDA Data Reveals

By Shuchi Shah

Dec 20, 2024

The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) conducts the federal agriculture census every five years through the National Agricultural Statistics Service (NASS), providing insights into shifts in this industry.

The most recent data from 2022 paints a picture of decline. Explore the map below for a state-by-state breakdown of percentage increase or decrease in Christmas tree harvests from 2017 to 2022.

Across the country, most states reported a decrease in the number of Christmas trees harvested compared to the previous survey. Tennessee experienced the sharpest drop, highlighting challenges within the local tree farming industry. Meanwhile, New Mexico stood out with the highest increase, defying the national trend.
